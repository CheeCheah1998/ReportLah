
# REPORTLAH – UI Theme & Design Recommendations (Leaflet Map)

## 1. Design Principles

Since REPORTLAH deals with:
- Anonymous reporting
- Corruption & sensitive data
- Price transparency for businesses
- Public map visualization

The theme should be:
- Trustworthy & credible
- Neutral & civic-oriented
- Calm, not sensational or aggressive
- Data-focused and professional

---

## 2. Recommended Color Palette

### Primary Colors
- **Deep Navy**: `#0F172A` – Base color, headers, navbars
- **Civic Blue**: `#1D4ED8` – Buttons, highlights, links

### Secondary Colors
- **Cool Gray**: `#64748B` – Text, secondary buttons
- **Light Gray Background**: `#F1F5F9` – Card backgrounds, panels

### Accent Colors (Minimal)
- **Warning Amber**: `#F59E0B` – Unverified reports
- **Soft Red**: `#DC2626` – Corruption markers, alerts

---

## 3. Map Marker Colors (Leaflet)

- 🏪 **Business Price Report** → Blue (`#1D4ED8`)
- 🚔 **Corruption Report** → Red (`#DC2626`)
- ⚠️ **Unverified Report** → Amber (`#F59E0B`)

> Red should be used sparingly to avoid emotional overload.  
> Blue gives credibility and neutrality. Amber signals caution.

---

## 4. Visual Style

- **Cards & Panels**: White background, soft shadows, rounded corners  
- **Typography**: Clean, sans-serif, legible  
- **Buttons**: Solid blue for primary, gray for secondary, minimal accent usage  
- **Hover Effects**: Subtle, non-distracting  
- **Map Style**: Neutral or light base tiles, Leaflet default or Mapbox Light tiles  

---

## 5. Alternative Theme (Dark / Investigative)

- Dark charcoal background  
- White text  
- Red for corruption markers  
- Blue for business markers  
- Only use dark mode if you want “investigative journalism” vibe  
- Avoid making it feel underground or political  

---

## 6. Theme Do’s & Don’ts

### ✅ Do’s
- Keep neutral and professional  
- Highlight data, not emotion  
- Use blue for credibility  
- Minimal use of red or amber for alerts  

### ❌ Don’ts
- Bright playful gradients  
- Heavy orange/red themes  
- Too colorful or flashy  
- Aggressive activist-style colors  

---

## 7. Leaflet Map Integration Notes

- Default map tile: Neutral light style (e.g., OpenStreetMap, Carto Light, or Mapbox Light)  
- Use clusters for dense markers  
- Marker popups should match UI theme (white card, shadow, rounded corners)  
- Filters and search panel: slate background or light gray to integrate with map  

---

## 8. Psychological Strategy

- Convey credibility and trust  
- Informational, not accusatory  
- Encourage reporting without fear  
- Present data visually clear and calm  

---

**Conclusion**:  
Use a **light civic-tech theme**: blue primary, soft gray background, minimal red/amber accents. Keep markers meaningful, interface clean, and feel trustworthy for both price transparency and corruption reporting on a Leaflet map.
